### Understanding What a VPN Does

VPNs, or Virtual Private Networks, act as secure tunnels for your internet traffic. Here’s a breakdown of their primary functions:

- **Encrypt your data**: Ensures that your internet activity remains private.
- **Change your IP address**: Masks your real location, providing anonymity.
- **Bypass geo-restrictions**: Access content that may be blocked in your region.

Any VPN worth its salt will offer these essential features.

## Key Features to Consider in a VPN

Before diving into my top picks, let’s cover some essential features you should consider:

### Security Protocols

Different VPN services offer various security protocols. Look for ones using:

- **OpenVPN**: The gold standard of VPN protocols, offering solid speed and security.
- **WireGuard**: A newer protocol designed for high speed and low latency.

### No-Log Policy

A "no-log" policy means the VPN doesn't keep records of your online activities. This is a crucial feature if privacy is your primary concern.

### Speed and Performance

You don’t want your internet speed to take a hit. Consider VPNs that maintain fast transfer rates, especially if you're streaming or gaming.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### 2. **Discounts and Pricing Plans Can Deceive**

You might find an attractive price for a short-term plan, but longer commitments often yield better savings. Consider what plan suits your needs.

### 3. **Personal Usage Matters**
