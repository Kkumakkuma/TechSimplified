## How Does a Home Network Work for Streaming?
A home network allows multiple devices to connect to the internet and communicate with each other. Here's a brief overview of how it works:

1. **Internet Service Provider (ISP)**: You subscribe to a broadband service that delivers internet to your home via cables or fiber optics.
   
2. **Modem**: The modem connects to your ISP and translates signals, enabling internet access.

3. **Router**: The router connects to the modem and distributes the internet connection to various devices via wired (Ethernet) or wireless (Wi-Fi) connections. 

4. **Devices**: This includes streaming devices like smart TVs, game consoles, laptops, or any other gadget that can access the internet for streaming content.

All of these elements need to work seamlessly together to ensure smooth streaming. A weak link in your setup can lead to frustrating buffering or low-quality streams.

## What’s the Difference Between Router Types?
When choosing a router, it’s essential to understand the two primary types: 

### Traditional Router
- **Use case**: Appropriately serves small homes with a few devices.
- **Speed**: Limited range and speed.
- **Cost**: Typically less expensive (around $100-$150).

### Mesh Router
- **Use case**: Best for larger homes or spaces where traditional routers struggle with dead zones.
- **Speed and Range**: Offers expanded coverage and stability across multiple devices.
- **Cost**: Can range from $200 to $600 depending on the system.

### Example
- **Linksys Velop Mesh**: Prices start around $400 for a pack of three nodes and is tailored for optimal performance in large spaces.
- **ASUS RT-AX86U**: A fantastic option for gaming and streaming, priced about $300, making it a serious contender for serious streamers.

1. **Buffering Issues**:
   - **Solution**: If you experience constant buffering, try resetting your router. A simple restart can sometimes clear up speed issues. Additionally, I recommend checking that no other devices are hogging bandwidth during peak streaming times.
   
2. **Weak Wi-Fi Signal**:
   - **Solution**: I faced connectivity issues in my home office where the signal was weak. Investing in a TP-Link RE450 WiFi Range Extender ($80) dramatically improved my streaming quality in that space. 

3. **Device Connectivity Problems**:
   - **Solution**: When devices can’t connect to the network, I found that sometimes the solution lies in the router settings. Adjusting the channel frequency in the router settings can greatly enhance device connectivity.
