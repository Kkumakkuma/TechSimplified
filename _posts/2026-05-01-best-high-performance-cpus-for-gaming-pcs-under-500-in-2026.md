## What to Consider Before Buying

When purchasing a CPU for gaming, consider the following:

- **Compatibility**: Always check for motherboard compatibility, especially between Intel and AMD.
- **Cooling Solutions**: Mid-range CPUs often don’t include coolers, so budget a decent cooler if you plan to push performance.
- **Future-proofing**: Aim for something with at least 6 cores and 12 threads to handle future gaming titles efficiently.

### What Most Guides Get Wrong

Despite various guides available online, here are three points where I frequently see misinformation.

- **“More cores are always better.”**: While having more cores can help with certain tasks, gaming performance often benefits more from higher clock speeds than sheer core count. Why this matters: For example, while 12 cores sound nice, a 6-core CPU with a higher clock speed often outperforms it in gaming scenarios due to better single-threaded performance.
  
- **“Integrated graphics are worthless.”**: Many guides downplay integrated graphics, but the latest Intel CPUs, such as the Core i5-13600K, offer competent graphics that can handle light gaming and are especially useful if you're waiting to buy a dedicated graphics card. Why this matters: It allows gamers to enjoy gaming while waiting for the GPU market to stabilize, as was the case earlier in 2026 when GPU prices peaked.

- **“Overclocking is necessary for best performance.”**: Not all gaming setups benefit significantly from overclocking; many CPUs operate well within their stock specifications. Why this matters: I learned from my own experience with the Ryzen 5 7600 that real-world performance amounted to a negligible difference unless you’re pushing top-tier games at ultra settings.
