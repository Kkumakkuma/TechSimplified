## What to Look for in a Portable Charger

When selecting a portable charger, there are several key factors to consider:

### Battery Capacity

- **Measured in mAh (milliamp hours)**: Higher capacity means more charges for your devices.
- For example, a charger rated at 20,000mAh can charge an iPhone 14 about 4-5 times.

### Portability

- Ideally, you want a charger that is lightweight and compact.
- Look for models that can easily fit into your bag or pocket without adding significant weight.

### Charging Speed

- Pay attention to wattage (W) output; the higher the wattage, the faster the charging.
- A charger with a 20W output can charge your device much quicker than one with only a 5W output.

### Number of Ports

- Consider how many devices you might need to charge.
- Some chargers feature multiple USB ports to charge several devices simultaneously, which can be a lifesaver during group trips.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How fast can portable chargers recharge devices?

A: Charging speed depends on the output power (measured in watts). Models like RAVPower’s 60W PD charger will recharge devices significantly faster than standard chargers.

### Q: Are there cheaper alternatives to these brands?

### Q: Can portable chargers be taken on airplanes?

A: Most portable chargers are allowed in carry-on luggage, but batteries exceeding 100Wh generally require approval. Always check current TSA regulations before you fly.

### Q: How much should I expect to spend on a portable charger?

A: Prices can range from about $20 for basic models to over $50 for more advanced models with higher capacities and faster charging capabilities.

### Q: Do I need a USB-C charger?

A: If your devices support USB-C, it’s advisable to get a portable charger with USB-C output for faster charging and versatility.
