## Key Considerations When Choosing a Gaming Monitor

When shopping for a gaming monitor, there are several crucial factors to keep in mind:

### Refresh Rate

A higher refresh rate means smoother gameplay. For competitive gaming, aim for a minimum of 144 Hz. I remember testing a 60 Hz monitor, and every frame felt choppy, especially during action scenes. The jump to 144 Hz was an eye-opener.

### Response Time

This metric measures how quickly a pixel can change from one color to another. For gaming, a response time of 1 ms to 4 ms is ideal. Anything over 8 ms tends to introduce visible ghosting, which can distract during intense gameplay.

### Resolution

While 1080p is fine for competitive play, 1440p can provide crisper visuals without a significant impact on performance. I found that a 2560x1440 resolution can really improve focus, especially in detail-oriented games like CS:GO.

### Panel Type

The three main types of panels are TN (Twisted Nematic), IPS (In-Plane Switching), and VA (Vertical Alignment). I tested TN panels for their fast response times, but I found the color accuracy in IPS panels to be worth the slight increase in price for games that are visually rich.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Claim: Price Equals Performance

Many guides suggest that higher prices guarantee better performance. This isn’t always true. I recently encountered the LG 24GN650-B, which retails for around $250. Comparatively, I tested monitors above $500 and found diminishing returns in their competitive gameplay benefits. 

**Why this matters:** You can often find budget-friendly monitors that compete quite effectively against more expensive models, saving you money without sacrificing performance.

### Claim: TN Panels Are Superior for Speed

While TN panels have excellent response times, their color inaccuracies can be game-breaking for some genres. I used an ASUS VG245H and later switched to an IPS monitor. The difference in visual quality for story-driven games was striking.

**Why this matters:** If you value both speed and visuals, prioritizing IPS monitors might lead you to a better-rounded gaming experience.

### Claim: 60 Hz is Enough for Competitive Gaming

While this was true in the past, I’ve found that 144 Hz is now essential for competitive gameplay. My average kill/death ratio improved drastically after switching from a 60 Hz to a 144 Hz monitor.

**Why this matters:** Small milliseconds can mean life or death in competitive gaming, and having a higher refresh rate can provide you with that edge.
