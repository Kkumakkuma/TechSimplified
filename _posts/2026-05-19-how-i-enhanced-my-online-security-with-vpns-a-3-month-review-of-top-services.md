## How Do VPNs Work?

VPNs function by creating encrypted connections between your device and the internet. When you activate a VPN, it routes your internet traffic through a server run by the VPN provider, masking your IP address and encrypting your data.

- **Encryption:** With military-grade encryption (AES-256, for example), a VPN safeguards your data against prying eyes. This means that even if someone intercepts your traffic, deciphering it requires formidable technical expertise.
  
- **IP Masking:** When connected to a VPN server, your actual IP address is masked behind one assigned by the VPN. This spoofing helps protect your online activities from being traced back to you.
  
- **Remote Access:** Whether you’re traveling or simply working from home, a VPN provides secure remote access to your network. This is critical for those handling sensitive information, such as corporate emails or personal identification details.

VPNs shield your activities on public networks, which are prime targets for cybercriminals. As I learned through testing, understanding how these features interplay significantly influences the overall effectiveness of a VPN in enhancing online security.
