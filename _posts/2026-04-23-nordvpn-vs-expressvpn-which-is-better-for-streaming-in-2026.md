## Understanding the VPN Landscape for Streaming

### Why Streaming with a VPN Matters

Streaming platforms like Netflix, Hulu, and Disney+ often have region-specific content. This means what I could access in Seoul was quite limited compared to, say, the U.S. market. Here are some benefits of using a VPN for streaming:

- **Access to Global Content**: With a VPN, you can bypass geographical restrictions and access a wider range of content.
- **Enhanced Privacy**: VPNs ensure your streaming habits stay private from your ISP.
- **Improved Speed**: A good VPN can actually improve your connection speed by lessening congestion during peak hours.

### Key Features to Look For 

When evaluating VPNs for streaming, consider the following critical factors:

- **Unblocking Capabilities**: The ability to bypass geo-blocks for various streaming services.
- **Speed**: A fast and stable connection is essential for high-quality streaming.
- **Server Locations**: More server locations mean greater flexibility in accessing different content.

## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### 1. Implying All VPNs Are the Same

Many reviews skip the nuances between VPNs and suggest any will do. In my experience, not all VPNs efficiently bypass streaming blocks or maintain speed.

### 2. Overlooking Service Quality

It’s not just about speed and unblocking; who you choose also affects customer support. I’ve reached out to both NordVPN and ExpressVPN, and I found that ExpressVPN’s support team was more responsive.

### 3. Neglecting Device Compatibility

With multiple devices in my household, I learned that some VPNs might have limitations on simultaneous connections. While NordVPN allows up to six devices, ExpressVPN capped me at five.

After thoroughly testing both, I would say both NordVPN and ExpressVPN offer great value for streaming, but your choice should align with your priorities:
