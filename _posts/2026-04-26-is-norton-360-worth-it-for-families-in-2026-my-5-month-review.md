## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Overemphasis on Price

Many guides suggest that price is the main factor when choosing an antivirus program. However, in my experience, features often outweigh costs. My $89.99 investment in Norton provided peace of mind that I couldn't put a price on.

Why this matters: If you focus solely on the lowest price, you might miss out on crucial features like real-time protection or parental controls, making the product less effective and potentially more costly in the long run.

### Ignoring Usability

Some reviews tend to overlook the user experience. Norton’s interface is simple and easy to use, which I truly appreciated when managing my kids' devices. Complicated setups can deter less tech-savvy users.

Why this matters: A complicated setup can lead to misuse or avoidance of needed security measures, especially by families who may not be tech-savvy.

### Misunderstanding Performance Impact

It's common advice that antivirus programs will slow down your devices significantly. While I did notice some slight lagging during intensive tasks, the overall impact was minimal. In fact, Norton updated its software during my testing period, which improved performance.

Why this matters: Understanding performance impact can guide you in selecting a program without unnecessary trials and errors based on misleading information.
